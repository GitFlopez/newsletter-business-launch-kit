### Master Issue Template

**SECTION 1 — HOOK (200-300 words)**
Open with ONE of:
- A specific moment: "Three years ago, I was 10 minutes from the worst performance review of my career when my manager said something that changed how I run every 1:1."
- A counterintuitive claim: "The single best thing you can do for your promotion case is stop talking about your technical skills."
- A question your reader is already asking: "What do executives actually think about IT? I finally asked one."

Then: 2 sentences that promise what they'll get if they keep reading.

**SECTION 2 — BODY (700-1,000 words)**
For essays: 3-5 specific points, each grounded in a real example. End each point with "The move:" — the specific thing to do.
For tutorials: Numbered steps. Each step: what to do + why it works + what failure looks like.
For case studies: Setup → decision point → outcome → what you'd take from it.

Always end body with: "The bottom line:" — 1-2 sentences of distilled wisdom.

**SECTION 3 — CTA (50-100 words)**
Pick ONE based on weekly goal:
- *List growth:* "If this was useful, forward it to one IT manager in your network who's stuck. That's how this newsletter grows."
- *Upgrade:* "The [specific template mentioned in issue] is in the paid archive. Upgrade to get it: [link]"
- *Community:* "What's your version of this situation? Reply or join the paid community discussion: [link]"
