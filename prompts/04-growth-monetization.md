# Prompt 4 — Growth Engine + Monetization Roadmap

**From 0 to monetized: the referral system, cross-promo machine, social funnel, sponsor kit, and revenue ladder.**

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a newsletter growth expert who has scaled newsletters from 0 to 10,000 subscribers using organic and partnership strategies. You know exactly how to build systems that grow a newsletter without paid ads and monetize each subscriber tier.

MY NEWSLETTER:
- Name: [NEWSLETTER NAME]
- Niche statement: [PASTE FROM PROMPT 1]
- Current subscriber count: [or "0 — launching"]
- Current monthly revenue: [or "$0 — pre-launch"]
- 12-month revenue goal: [$X/month]
- Paid tier price: [$X/month or $X/year]
- Platform: [Substack / Ghost / Beehiiv / ConvertKit]
- My strongest distribution channel right now: [LinkedIn / Twitter-X / email / word of mouth / none yet]

DELIVER:

1. REFERRAL PROGRAM
Full referral program built for my platform. Include:
- 3-tier milestone rewards (e.g., 3 referrals → digital download, 10 → 1 month paid free, 25 → VIP group access)
- Referral ask email (sent at Day 21 — after reader has seen value)
- Milestone reward delivery emails (1 per tier)
- Social share copy (3 tweet/post templates readers paste verbatim)
- Where to place the referral ask within each issue (section + placement recommendation)

2. CROSS-PROMOTION SYSTEM
Everything I need to partner with 5-10 complementary newsletters.
- Ideal partner profile: 3 criteria for a good cross-promo match (size, audience overlap, content type)
- Outreach email template (cold pitch to newsletter authors I don't know)
- Partnership terms template (swap: each mentions the other in 1 issue)
- 50-word blurb template readers see in a partner newsletter
- 3 specific newsletter categories that are perfect cross-promo partners for MY niche

3. SOCIAL FUNNEL
Platform-specific growth engine for [my strongest channel].
For Twitter/X:
- Pinned tweet / profile bio formula
- 3 thread structures that consistently drive newsletter subscriptions (format + first line of each)
- Optimal posting cadence for newsletter growth
- 1 engagement play per week (poll / quote-tweet / thread reply) that builds authority in my niche
For LinkedIn (if chosen):
- Profile summary formula for newsletter promotion
- 3 post formats that drive subscribe clicks
- Newsletter article strategy (native LinkedIn vs. link-out)

4. SPONSOR KIT
Everything I need to land my first sponsor at 1,000+ subscribers.
- Rate card formula (CPM-based + flat rate for my audience size)
- Sponsor pitch email (cold outreach to brands in my niche)
- Sponsorship package tiers: Solo mention / Dedicated send / Exclusive quarter
- Sponsor FAQ (5 questions brands always ask)
- Where to find my first 5 potential sponsors (specific directories or communities)

5. MONETIZATION LADDER
A 12-month revenue roadmap showing exactly when and how to activate each tier:
Month 1-2: [specific action] → Goal: [specific milestone]
Month 3-4: [specific action] → Goal: [specific milestone]
Month 5-6: [specific action] → Goal: [specific milestone]
Month 7-9: [specific action] → Goal: [specific milestone]
Month 10-12: [specific action] → Goal: [specific milestone]

Revenue model: show me the math to reach $[12-month goal] combining paid subs + sponsorships + one product.
```

---

## How to Use This Output

- Set up the referral program before Issue #1 — Beehiiv and Sparkloop integrate natively; Substack has built-in referrals
- Send the cross-promo outreach to 3 newsletters in your first month — it's the fastest way to 500 subscribers
- Publish the social funnel content on autopilot — schedule 2 weeks ahead using Buffer or Typefully
- Don't approach sponsors until you have 1,000+ subscribers and 2+ months of open rate data
- Use the monetization ladder as a quarterly OKR — one revenue activation per quarter

---

## Pro Tips

- **Referral programs work at 500+ subscribers.** Before that, direct asks convert better.
- **Cross-promos are 10× more efficient than social posting.** 1 partner mention = 50-200 subscribers vs. 5-15 from a viral tweet.
- **Your first sponsor will be a small brand in your niche.** Don't aim for Fortune 500 — aim for a tool, software, or service your readers already use.
- **The monetization ladder is a commitment device.** Share it publicly in Issue #1 — "I'm building this in public, here's the plan." Accountability drives execution.
