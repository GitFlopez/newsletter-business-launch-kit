# Prompt 2 — Paid Subscription Pitch + Launch Welcome Sequence

**Everything a reader sees in the first 14 days — from landing page to first upgrade ask.**

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a conversion copywriter who has written landing pages and email sequences for 100+ successful paid newsletters. Every word you write is designed to convert browsers to free subscribers and free subscribers to paid.

MY NEWSLETTER:
- Name: [NEWSLETTER NAME]
- Niche statement (from Prompt 1): [PASTE YOUR NICHE STATEMENT]
- My story/credentials: [2-3 sentences — why you are the right person to write this]
- Paid tier price: [$X/month or $X/year — or ask me to recommend one]
- What paid subscribers get that free subscribers don't: [or say "recommend for me"]
- Tone: [professional / conversational / punchy / academic]

DELIVER:

1. LANDING PAGE COPY
Complete copy for a Substack or Ghost about page. Sections:
- Headline (10 words max, benefit-first)
- Subheadline (20 words max, expands the promise)
- The Problem (3 sentences — describe the reader's frustration in their own words)
- What You'll Get (3 bullets, format: "[Specific result] — [how you deliver it]")
- About the Author (75 words — credentials + personal hook + why you write this)
- Social Proof Hook (2 options for future testimonials — placeholder format)
- Paid Tier Pitch (50 words — what paid unlocks + price justification)
- FAQ (4 questions: "Who is this for?", "How often do you publish?", "What if I don't like it?", "Is it worth paying for?")
- CTA (3 variations: subscribe free / upgrade now / gift a subscription)

2. 5-EMAIL WELCOME SEQUENCE
Write all 5 emails. For each: Subject line + Preview text + Full email body.

- Email 1 (Day 0 — send immediately): Confirmation + your best piece of content
- Email 2 (Day 1): Your story — why you started this, what changed for you
- Email 3 (Day 3): A quick win — something they can do or think about today
- Email 4 (Day 7): Social proof + community invitation ("you're not alone")
- Email 5 (Day 14): The upgrade ask — paid pitch, specific benefit, limited framing

3. PAID TIER PRICING RATIONALE
If I haven't set a price: recommend a monthly price, annual price (with discount %), and the single sentence that justifies both. If I have set a price: write the sentence that makes $[X] feel like a no-brainer.
```

---

## How to Use This Output

- Paste the landing page copy directly into Substack About or Ghost page — customize the author section and social proof
- Set up the email automation in Substack/ConvertKit/Beehiiv before your first subscriber arrives
- Email 5 is your most important — test 2 versions of the subject line over your first 100 subscribers
- The paid tier pricing rationale becomes your upgrade banner copy

---

## Pro Tips

- **The problem section is your most powerful copy.** If readers say "how did you know that?", you have it right.
- **Email 1 should deliver a win, not just say thanks.** Link to your single best piece of content immediately.
- **Email 5 timing matters.** Day 14 is optimal — readers have seen enough value but haven't gone cold.
- **Don't launch with a paid tier.** Get 250 free subscribers first, then turn on paid with Email 5 as your trigger.
